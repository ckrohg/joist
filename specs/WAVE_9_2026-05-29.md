## 0. TL;DR

Three load-bearing findings between 2026-05-26 and 2026-05-29 (a three-day delta, which the frontier outran):

1. **Anthropic published the canonical agentic-design pattern on 2026-03-24** — [harness-design-long-running-apps](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/harness-design-long-running-apps). The convergence shape across Cursor 3.5, Replit Agent 4, v0, and Lovable is **generator + evaluator** (not three agents). The evaluator has Playwright MCP and navigates the live page. Key insight: *"agents reliably skew positive when grading their own work. Separating roles is the lever."*

2. **Cited evidence that naive critique loops degrade output.** Two papers converge on the same finding:
   - VisRefiner (Feb 2026) — naive refinement of baseline models *makes them worse*. Mitigation: **Forced Optimization** — only accept refinement steps that strictly improve the reward signal.
   - Patterns / Cell Press 2025 — 700 SDXL↔LLaVA trajectories at every sampling parameter converged to **12 dominant motifs** ("visual elevator music"). Unattended self-critique collapses to clichés.

3. **Rationale-bearing rules + 3-tier substrate is the 2026 H1 state of the art for taste capture.** Anthropic's own 2026 constitution grew from 2,700 → 23,000 words specifically by shifting from rule lists to explanation-with-rationale. State of the art is three layers: **constitution + rule list with rationale + cached exemplar pack**. Our current `preference_memory` is layer 2 only and lacks the rationale field.

**Bottom line:** v0.85 is the substrate. v0.9 becomes the *taste-improving loop* on top of it. None of v0.85 is invalidated; this is purely additive.

## 1. The three findings (deep)

### 1.1 The Anthropic generator/evaluator harness (March 24, 2026)

**Source:** [anthropic.com/engineering/harness-design-long-running-apps](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/harness-design-long-running-apps). Verified against [InfoQ writeup](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/04/anthropic-three-agent-harness-ai/) and [Epsilla deconstruction](https://www.epsilla.com/blogs/anthropic-harness-engineering-multi-agent-gan-architecture).

**The shape:**

```
generator (taste-loaded skill)
  → render to a real DOM via Playwright
  → evaluator (skeptical prompt, four scoring axes)
  → structured critique back to generator
  → N ≤ 15 iterations OR human gate
```

**The four scoring axes** (Anthropic's frontend evaluator rubric):
1. **Design quality** — coherent whole vs sum-of-parts
2. **Originality** — custom decisions vs generic patterns
3. **Craft** — typography, spacing, contrast
4. **Functionality** — does it actually work

**Key implementation notes:**
- Two agents, not three. The planner was added *only* for full-stack work, not pure frontend.
- The evaluator navigates the live page via Playwright MCP — screenshots, studies, then critiques. Not just a static snapshot.
- 5-15 iterations per generation; quality plateaus, not monotone.
- Runs up to 4 hours in Anthropic's reference implementation. For Joist, we cap at 5 iterations (cost discipline).

**Why this matters to Joist:** This is the canonical pattern, published by the same vendor whose model + Skills format we ship on. Maps cleanly onto our v0.85 architecture: generator = current build agent; evaluator = new sibling skill with Playwright MCP pointed at the rendered Elementor preview iframe; Plan Mode UI is the natural HITL gate between iterations.

**What's NOT in the harness** (verified by reading Anthropic's [frontend-design SKILL.md](https://github.com/anthropics/claude-code/blob/main/plugins/frontend-design/skills/frontend-design/SKILL.md)): the Skill itself is single-pass — ~400 tokens of aesthetic guidance (banned fonts list including Inter / Roboto / Arial / Space Grotesk; commit to a direction). The loop is the *harness wrapping the skill*. Our `/elementor-build` should follow the same separation: skill = taste prompt; harness = orchestration.

### 1.2 Forced Optimization — the non-optional monotonicity gate

**Source 1: VisRefiner (Feb 2026)** — [arxiv 2602.05998](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.05998v1). Difference-driven training for screenshot-to-code self-refinement. Headline: VisRefiner-7B hits 85.6 avg on Design2Code-HARD with Block-Match 78.1 (+21.5 over GPT-4o). The critical detail: **authors explicitly observed that naive refinement in baseline models often degrades output.** Their contribution is the training paradigm that prevents regression.

**Source 2: Patterns / Cell Press 2025** — [PMC12827715](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12827715/). 700 autonomous SDXL↔LLaVA trajectories over 100 iterations at 7 temperatures. All converged to **12 dominant motifs** ("stormy lighthouses, palatial interiors, gothic cathedrals"). Convergence held across all sampling parameters. Authors term it "visual elevator music."

**The implication for any loop Joist builds:**

```
if critique(after_edit) < critique(before_edit):
    refuse_commit()  # this is non-optional
```

The Patterns finding goes further: even monotonically-improving loops will collapse to generic motifs if run unattended. The human keystone (Plan Mode UI approval) is the second non-optional gate.

**This becomes failure-mode constraint #21 (Forced Optimization) and #22 (anti-cliché diversity check) — see §7.**

### 1.3 Three-tier taste substrate

**Source: TASTE dataset (May 2026)** — [arxiv 2605.20731](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.20731). Ten professional designers rated four T2I models across nine criteria. A small pairwise-difference model trained on TASTE hit 0.611 macro agreement vs 0.741 single-rater ceiling. **Zero-shot VLM judges all scored below 0.55.** Implication: raw VLM cannot be trusted to autonomously decide "is this on-brand?" until you've trained a pairwise diff model on your own accept/reject log.

**Source: Anthropic 2026 Constitution** — [anthropic.com/news/claudes-constitution](https://www.anthropic.com/news/claudes-constitution). Grew from 2,700 → 23,000 words and shifted from rule-list to *explanation-with-rationale*. Models generalize from "we avoid stock-photo gradients because we sell handmade ceramics," not from `forbid_phrase: gradient`.

**Source: Personalized-Agents Survey** — [arxiv 2602.22680](https://arxiv.org/html/2602.22680v2). Taxonomy: (expression: explicit vs implicit) × (semantic: behavioral vs topical). Three subjective-taste families: response-based reward factorization, natural-language preference summaries, persona-CoT.

**Source: Mem0 state-of-memory 2026** — [mem0.ai/blog/state-of-ai-agent-memory-2026](https://mem0.ai/blog/state-of-ai-agent-memory-2026). Five production failure modes beyond our existing five:
1. State changes treated as *replacement* not *evolution* — a brand pivot becomes confidently-wrong overwrite
2. Cross-session identity breaks with multi-device / anonymous use
3. **Blocking memory writes on response = #1 production footgun** (use async)
4. Temporal queries: -25% accuracy at 10M tokens
5. Procedural memory (workflows) distinct from factual memory

**The 2026 H1 state of the art is three layers:**

1. **Constitution** — ~50 high-level principles with rationale, versioned. Agency-level default + per-site override. Loaded into the cached system prefix. (For Joist: `joist.constitution.md`.)
2. **Rule list** — site-specific rules with **rationale + provenance** (our current preference_memory, plus the rationale + `superseded_by` fields we're adding in v0.9).
3. **Exemplar pack** — 5-20 approved design references as cached message-history examples. Refreshed on approval. **This is new.**

## 3. Architecture deltas

### 3.1 Skill bundle becomes a 2-skill harness

**Current (v0.85 plan):** `/elementor-build`, `/elementor-edit`, `/elementor-audit` — three skills, each single-pass.

**New (v0.9 plan):**

- `/elementor-build` (generator) — taste-loaded, produces a Plan; Plan goes to Plan Mode UI for approval
- `/elementor-critique` (evaluator, NEW) — Playwright MCP, AesEval rubric, skeptical prompt, returns `{score, regions:[{bbox, severity, comment}], verdict: accept | revise | reject}`
- `/elementor-edit` — unchanged at v0.9 (surgical edit; uses generator+critique under the hood for non-trivial edits)
- `/elementor-audit` — unchanged at v0.9

Both `/elementor-build` and `/elementor-edit` invoke the harness pattern: generator → render → critique → revise or commit. Bounded at 5 iterations (cost discipline; see [[failure-mode-constraints]] #23).

### 3.2 New REST endpoint: `POST /joist/v1/critique`

**Request:**
```json
{
  "site_id": "string",
  "page_id": "int",
  "screenshot_url": "string (presigned to Joist's render service)",
  "brand_tokens": {...},
  "forbidden": ["string"],
  "rubric": "aeseval | joist_brand | both",
  "max_iterations_remaining": "int"
}
```

**Response:**
```json
{
  "score": 0.0-1.0,
  "verdict": "accept" | "revise" | "reject",
  "regions": [{"bbox": [x,y,w,h], "severity": "low|medium|high", "comment": "string"}],
  "score_delta_vs_previous": "number | null",
  "iteration_budget_remaining": "int",
  "anti_cliche_check": {"similarity_to_recent": 0.0-1.0, "flagged": "bool"}
}
```

Paired with the still-pending `POST /joist/v1/preview/render` endpoint (carried over from the v0.85 follow-up list).

### 3.3 Three-tier taste substrate

```
[joist.constitution.md]           <-- agency default; ~50 rationale-bearing principles
   |
   +-- site_override.constitution.md  <-- per-site overrides (optional)
   |
   v
[preference_memory rules]         <-- current substrate; v0.9 adds rationale + superseded_by
   |
   v
[exemplar_pack]                   <-- NEW: 5-20 approved designs as cached message-history
```

All three layers are loaded into the cached prefix on every generation call. The 5-min cache TTL means tight batching per site (already established in W6c). At Opus 4.7 rates, the three-tier prefix costs ~$0.15 cache write + ~$0.0125 per read; a Plan Mode burst of 8 prompts is ~$0.25 total.

### 3.4 Forced Optimization gate on Document::save

In `DocumentWriter.php`:

```
before_score = critique(current_page_state)
proposed_state = apply_patch(...)
after_score = critique(proposed_state)
if after_score < before_score:
    raise ForcedOptimizationRejection(before, after, deltas)
# else commit
```

This is non-optional per failure-mode constraint #21. Bypassable only via an explicit human override in Plan Mode UI (with an audit log entry).

### 3.5 Anti-cliché diversity check

In the critique pipeline:

```
similarity = cosine_sim(
  embed(proposed_render),
  embed(last_N_committed_renders_for_site)
)
if similarity > 0.92:  # tunable threshold
    verdict = "revise"
    flag = "anti_cliche_diversity"
```

Cited evidence: the Patterns paper found 700/700 trajectories converged to 12 motifs at every sampling parameter. Without this check, our generator will drift to population-mean Elementor aesthetics over time — exactly the slop we're fighting.

### 3.6 Adopt Glance MCP pattern

[Glance MCP (DebugBase)](https://github.com/DebugBase/glance) is a production-shipped MCP server giving Claude Code real Chromium via Playwright/CDP. 30 tools including `browser_screenshot` (base64 to Claude) and explicit visual regression baseline/compare. Claims 97% pass rate over 300+ test steps in production.

**Decision:** Adopt either Glance MCP directly (if license / dependency-shape works) or implement the pattern in-house (a thin MCP server wrapping Playwright). Do **not** build Playwright orchestration from scratch — that battle has been fought.

### 3.7 AGENTS.md emission

When Joist builds a site, it emits an `AGENTS.md` at the site root capturing the per-site brand rules + constitution overrides. This file is read by Cursor / Claude Code / Codex / Aider downstream — cross-tool standard convergence in May 2026.

Free win, ~80 LOC.

## 4. Failure-mode constraints additions (#21-#24)

**#21 — Forced Optimization gate.** Refuse any Document::save where post-edit critique score is strictly less than pre-edit. Cited: VisRefiner (Feb 2026) empirical observation that naive refinement degrades baseline models; Patterns / Cell Press 2025 evidence that unattended loops collapse to clichés.

**#22 — Anti-cliché diversity check.** Reject (verdict: revise) any proposed render whose cosine similarity to the last N committed renders for the site exceeds threshold (default 0.92). Cited: Patterns paper, 700 trajectories converging to 12 motifs.

**#23 — Bounded critique iteration (N=5 default).** Cap critique loops at 5 iterations and fall back to last-best on plateau. Anthropic's reference runs up to 4 hours; we don't have that budget envelope. Cited: Anthropic harness post + our own cost discipline (failure-mode #9).

**#24 — No autonomous raw-VLM slop filter.** Zero-shot VLM judges score below 0.55 macro agreement on TASTE; pairwise diff models reach 0.611 vs 0.741 single-rater ceiling. Until Joist has trained a pairwise judge on its own accept/reject log, raw VLM critique drives `verdict: revise` only, never autonomous commit. Cited: TASTE dataset (May 2026).

Full updated catalogue in [[failure-mode-constraints]].

## 5. Preference_memory v0.9 refactor

Net changes to the substrate:

- **`rationale` field** on every Rule. Required when promoting via SlopFeedback; optional for manually-authored rules but warned in CLI.
- **`superseded_by` field** on every Rule. When a rule is replaced (brand pivot), the old rule stores the new rule's ID; the renderer prefers `superseded_by` when traversing.
- **`last_reinforced_at` timestamp.** Confidence decays linearly over 90 days unless re-reinforced. Stops 2025-era preferences hardening into law.
- **Promotion gate tightening.** SlopFeedback rules now require 3 rejections **across distinct sessions / days**, not within one Plan Mode burst. Mitigates "bad day" overfitting.
- **BASIL-lite Bayesian consistency check.** Before promoting a SlopFeedback rule, sample 3 prior approvals from exemplar_pack and verify the new rejection is consistent. Reject promotion if not.
- **Async writes audit.** Move all preference_memory writes off the agent response path. Per Mem0, blocking writes on response is the #1 production footgun.
- **Promotion rate limit.** Max 1 auto-promotion per 10 minutes per session. Prevents rage-clicking from poisoning the rule store.

LOC estimate for the prefmem v0.9 work: ~280 PHP + acceptance test additions.

## 6. Active-learning UX (Plan Mode addition)

**The rule: ask only when information-value exceeds friction cost.**

Concretely:

- When the planner detects two plausible directions with conflicting prior signal (site rules say "minimal" but this section needs prominence), surface a *one-time choice card*: "For this hero section, do you want A (quiet/editorial) or B (loud/conversion-focused)?"
- The result writes a `layout_rule` with section-scope and rationale capturing the user's reason for the choice.
- Do **not** offer A/B on routine decisions — SAGE's clarification-cost lesson.
- Max 1 choice card per Plan Mode session. Frequency >2 trains users to tune out.
- Every choice card pairs with "ask me again later" — never let one A/B answer set a permanent rule. Promote to rule only after 2 consistent same-direction answers.

UX surface: new `ChoiceCard.jsx` component in `plugin/src/admin-app/components/`. Integrates with the existing Plan Mode flow as an optional pre-approval interstitial.

## 7. AesEval-Bench public targeting

[AesEval-Bench](https://arxiv.org/abs/2603.01083) (March 2026, ICLR'26 under review) is the closest thing to SWE-bench for design judgment quality. 4 dimensions × 12 indicators × 3 tasks (judgment / region selection / precise localization), ~4,500 QA pairs, 30K-example training set. GPT-5 leads at 72.5% judgment, 19.9% IoU on precise localization. Reasoning models showed no advantage.

**Joist commitment:** publish an AesEval-Bench score for our `/elementor-critique` evaluator at v1.0 launch. Becomes a credible-trust signal that no competitor has claimed. Pairs with the public failure-mode catalogue ([docs/FAILURE_MODES.md](../docs/FAILURE_MODES.md)) as the v1.0 positioning artifact set.

## 8. Cost envelope

**Per-Plan-Mode-session at Opus 4.7 (estimated):**
- Three-tier substrate cached prefix (~55K tokens cached): $0.15 write, $0.0125 per read
- Generator: 8 calls × ~3K input × ~2K output = ~$0.41
- Critic: 5 iterations × ~5K input (screenshot + brand context) × ~500 output = ~$0.19
- **Total: ~$0.75 per session** at Opus, ~$0.30 at Sonnet 4.6.

Within our existing per-build cost envelope (W6 documented $5/session copy cap, $10/session image cap; critique work fits inside).

**Per-critique-call latency:**
- Screenshot capture via Glance MCP: ~800ms
- Critic call (Opus 4.7 vision, 1024px downscale): ~2.5s
- Total: ~3.5s per iteration; ~17s for a 5-iteration capped loop.

**Don't pay for extended thinking on critique.** AesEval-Bench finding: o-class / Gemini-thinking models showed no judgment lift over non-reasoning. Default mode only.

## 10. Open questions / deferred decisions

1. **Glance MCP licensing.** Need to verify the GPL/MIT compatibility before adopting directly. Fallback: thin in-house wrapper around Playwright (~300 LOC).
2. **Anti-cliché threshold tuning.** 0.92 cosine is the starting heuristic; needs empirical validation against our own approval log once we have one.
3. **Pairwise judge bootstrap.** When do we have enough approve/reject signal to train a TASTE-style pairwise judge? Probably v0.95 or v1.0; defer the training pipeline spec.
4. **Constitution authorship.** Who writes the ~50 principles? Founder + brand-architect skill + first 5 customer sites probably converges to a draft.
5. **Should `/elementor-critique` invoke vision via Anthropic API directly, or route through our existing copy-gen CopyGenerator infrastructure?** Likely direct (different model selection + caching profile).
6. **AesEval-Bench reproduction.** Do we vendor their eval harness or just publish our score? Reproduction is the trust signal; publishing alone is a marketing artifact.
